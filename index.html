Evolution of the Philippine Constitution

Constitution

A part of a whole, coming together of distinct groups with the same principles and ideals.

1897 Constitution of Biak-na-Bato

  • Provisionary constitution during the revolution.
  • Promulgated on November 1, 1897, written by Isabelo Artacho and Felix Ferrer in Spanish.
  • Organs of the Government:
    • Supreme Council (President and four Department Secretaries: Interior, Foreign Affairs, Treasury, War)
    • Consejo Supremo de Gracia y Justicia (Supreme Council of Grace and Justice)
    • Asemblea de Representantes (Assembly of Representatives)
  • Never fully implemented due to the truce between Spain and the Philippine Revolutionary Army.

1899 Malolos Constitution

  • Drafted after the declaration of Philippine Independence.
  • Approved by Congress on November 29, 1898 and promulgated in January 1899.
  • Written in Spanish with 39 articles divided into 14 titles.
  • Never enforced due to the Philippine-American War.

1935 Commonwealth Constitution

  • Result of the Tydings-McDuffie Act.
  • Draft approved on March 25, 1935.
  • Governed the Philippines from 1935 to 1946.
  • Initially provided for a unicameral National Assembly; later changed to a bicameral legislature.

1973 Constitution (Marcos Era)

  • Martial law was declared before the convention was completed.
  • Legislative power vested in a unicameral National Assembly.
  • President could serve unlimited terms.
  • Major amendments occurred until 1984.

1987 Constitution (Post-Martial Law)

  • Drafted after the 1986 People Power Revolution.
  • Officially adopted on February 2, 1987.
  • Established a democratic, republican state with three branches of government.
  • Created three Constitutional Commissions:
    • Civil Service Commission (CSC)
    • Commission on Elections (COMELEC)
    • Commission on Audit (COA)

Attempts to Amend the 1987 Constitution

  • Methods: Constituent Assembly (Con-Ass), Constitutional Convention (Con-Con), People’s Initiative (PI).
  • Efforts by Joseph Estrada and Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo to initiate charter change.

Federalism Proposal

  • Supported by President Duterte in 2016.
  • Pros: Custom solutions, regional financial control.
  • Cons: Challenges in achieving unity, jurisdictional overlaps.

Preamble

Latin "preambulare" meaning "to walk before". It is an introduction that does not confer rights or impose obligations. It serves to explain the purpose and spirit of the Constitution.

Objective & Importance of Preamble

  • States origin and purpose.
  • Identifies the authors and intent of the Constitution.
  • Aids in interpretation of the document.

Evolution of Philippine Taxation

Taxation

Government mechanism to raise funds through compulsory financial charges (taxes) on citizens and businesses.

Spanish Era

  • Tributos collected to fund colonial expenses.
  • 1884: Tribute replaced by Cedula Personal.
  • Introduction of Urbana and Industria taxes.
  • Forced labor system (Polo y Servicio) required males to work 40 days a year (later reduced).
  • Widespread tax evasion and disorderly tax collection led to the Internal Revenue Law of 1904.

American Era

  • Introduced major sources of revenue including income tax (1914) and inheritance tax (1919).
  • 1932: National lottery established to raise funds.

Commonwealth Period

  • Higher income taxes and the introduction of residence tax (1940).
  • Economic decline during Japanese occupation.

Post-War to Present

  • 1946 onwards: Poor tax collection and corruption.
  • 1986: Aquino introduced the Value-Added Tax (VAT).
  • 1997: Ramos’ Comprehensive Tax Reform Program (CTRP).
  • Estrada's term was too short for major reforms.
  • Arroyo signed the Expanded Value-Added Tax (E-VAT) law (RA 9337).
  • Benigno Aquino III introduced the Sin Tax Reform for liquor and cigarettes.
  • President Duterte lowered income tax rates and increased excise taxes on petroleum and automobiles.
